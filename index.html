Malcolm Conway qualified in Advanced Craft and Stonemasonry from Glasgow College of Building and Printing in 1994, having served a rigorous four-year apprenticeship. In the three decades since, he has built one of Scotland's most trusted names in stone.

His work spans the nation's most celebrated buildings — Stirling Castle, the Wallace Monument, the Church of Holy Rude, the Scottish Parliament — and the homes and gardens of families across Stirling, Fife, Edinburgh and Glasgow who simply want the job done right.

Every project, large or small, is approached with the same care: the right stone, the right mortar, done once and done well.

What We Do

Services

Heritage & Listed Building Restoration

Sympathetic repair and restoration of historic properties using traditional techniques and period-appropriate materials. Fully conversant with Historic Scotland requirements and conservation standards.

Lime Mortar Repointing

Specialist repointing using traditional lime mortars to preserve and protect Victorian, Edwardian, and older stonework. Prevents moisture damage while maintaining the breathability historic buildings require.

New Build Stonework

New construction in natural stone — garages, gable ends, boundary walls, and structural elements built to match or complement existing period properties. Extensions that look like they were always there.

Windows, Cills & Entrances

Repair and replacement of sandstone window cills, door surrounds, entrance pillars, and threshold steps. Sourcing stone to match original material for a seamless, invisible repair.

Garden Features & Landscaping

Bespoke stone features for outdoor living — fire pits, seating areas, retaining walls, paving, and decorative stonework. Crafted to the same exacting standard as any structural work.

Chimneys, Steps & Walls

Taking down and rebuilding damaged chimneys, repairing exterior steps, and rebuilding boundary walls using original or matched stone. Structural integrity restored to last another century.
